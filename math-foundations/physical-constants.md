```math

```

## Useful Combinations

### Quantum Mechanics

```math
\hbar c = 197.327 \text{ MeV·fm} \approx 200 \text{ MeV·fm}
```

```math
\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} = 1.440 \text{ MeV·fm}
```

```math
\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_e} = 3.810 \text{ eV·Å}^2
```

### Relativity

```math
\frac{GM_\odot}{c^2} = 1.476 \text{ km}
```

```math
\frac{GM_\odot}{c^3} = 4.926 \text{ μs}
```

### Statistical Mechanics

```math
k_B = 8.617 \times 10^{-5} \text{ eV/K}
```

```math
\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_e k_B} = 4.42 \times 10^{-3} \text{ K·cm}^2
```

**The essential insight:** Physical constants set the scales of nature. The fine structure constant $\alpha \approx 1/137$ determines atomic physics; the proton-electron mass ratio $m_p/m_e \approx 1836$ sets nuclear vs. atomic scales; Boltzmann's constant connects microscopic energy to temperature. Memorizing a few key combinations ($\hbar c \approx 200$ MeV·fm, $k_BT \approx 1/40$ eV at room temperature, $a_0 \approx 0.5$ Å) enables quick estimates across all of physics.
